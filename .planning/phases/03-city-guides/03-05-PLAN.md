For Latin American fans, Toronto offers a safe, clean, and incredibly diverse urban experience with the iconic CN Tower, world-class museums, and a surprisingly warm summer. BMO Field is at Exhibition Place, accessible by streetcar from Union Station (509/510 route, 15 min). VIA Rail from Montreal (4.5h) and Ottawa (4.5h).

Budget: $70-120 CAD downtown hostels/budget hotels, $150-300 CAD downtown hotels, $80-130 CAD North York/Scarborough.

Latin American food in Kensington and along St. Clair West. Budget: $12-20 CAD casual, $30-60 CAD sit-down. Note: prices are in Canadian dollars (1 CAD ≈ 0.73 USD as of 2026).

- **weather**: June-July is Toronto's summer — 18-28C (64-82F), generally pleasant with sunshine. Can have humid heat waves (30C+). Occasional thunderstorms. Lake Ontario moderates temperatures. - **overview**: Vancouver is one of the world's most beautiful cities — mountains meet ocean with the North Shore peaks rising dramatically behind the downtown skyline. BC Place (home of the Whitecaps, 54,500 capacity with a retractable roof) will host World Cup matches. The metro area (~2.6M) is incredibly diverse with large Asian, South Asian, and growing Latin American communities. Vancouver is consistently ranked as one of the most livable cities in the world. For Latin American fans, Vancouver offers breathtaking natural scenery, world-class Asian food, and a relaxed West Coast vibe — though it's one of the most expensive cities in Canada. IMPORTANT: Like Toronto, most Latin American visitors need a Canadian eTA.

- **gettingThere**: Vancouver International (YVR) with direct flights from Mexico City, some seasonal Latin American routes, and excellent connections via US hubs. Canada Line SkyTrain connects YVR to downtown (25 min, $4.55-$10.30 CAD depending on zones/time). BC Place is downtown, walkable from most downtown hotels. VIA Rail Canadian transcontinental from Toronto (4 days, scenic but long). Amtrak Cascades from Seattle (4h).

- **gettingAround**: TransLink: SkyTrain (3 lines — Expo, Millennium, Canada), buses, SeaBus (ferry to North Vancouver). Compass Card for all transit. Downtown is very walkable and bikeable. Mobi bike share. Uber/Lyft. BC Place is in the heart of downtown — walkable from Gastown, Yaletown, West End, Chinatown. No car needed in the city center. Aquabus/False Creek Ferries for waterfront transport.

- **neighborhoods**: Downtown/West End (central, walkable, close to BC Place, Stanley Park), Gastown (historic, trendy, restaurants), Yaletown (upscale, waterfront, converted warehouses), Kitsilano (beach, relaxed, mountain views), Commercial Drive (bohemian, diverse, Italian roots, growing Latin community). Budget: $100-180 CAD downtown budget, $200-400 CAD downtown hotels, $80-130 CAD Burnaby/New Westminster (SkyTrain accessible).

- **foodAndDrink**: Vancouver has arguably the best Asian food in North America. Sushi (far superior to most US cities — try sushi on Robson Street), dim sum in Richmond, ramen on Robson, pho everywhere. Japadog (Japanese hot dog — only in Vancouver). Granville Island Public Market for local food and crafts. Craft beer scene (33 Acres, Brassneck). Pacific salmon. Budget: $13-22 CAD casual, $35-70 CAD sit-down. Commercial Drive has affordable, eclectic options.

- **safety**: Extremely safe city. The Downtown Eastside (around Hastings and Main) has a visible homelessness/drug crisis — avoid walking through this specific area, especially at night. Everything else (Stanley Park, Gastown, Yaletown, Kitsilano, West End) is very safe. Emergency: 911. Wilderness: if hiking, be aware of bears and trail conditions.

Stanley Park, Granville Island, and the mountain/ocean backdrop make this one of the most scenic World Cup cities in history. For Latin American fans: Vancouver may feel more "Asian-Pacific" than familiar, but the natural beauty and safety are unmatched. Tips: 15-18%.
