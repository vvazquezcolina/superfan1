**MIAMI** (~1500 words ES, ~1500 words EN):

- **overview**: Miami is THE gateway to Latin America in the United States. Hard Rock Stadium in Miami Gardens (65,000 capacity, home of the Dolphins) will host World Cup matches. The Greater Miami area (~6.1M metro) is ~70% Hispanic — it's the most Latin American city in the US by far. Spanish is not just spoken here, it's often the primary language. Cuban, Colombian, Venezuelan, Argentine, Nicaraguan, Haitian communities make this the most culturally diverse Latin city in North America. For any Latin American fan, Miami IS home turf. Beach culture, nightlife, incredible food from every corner of Latin America, year-round tropical weather.

- **gettingThere**: Miami International (MIA) is the premier gateway to/from Latin America with direct flights from virtually every Latin American capital. Fort Lauderdale (FLL, 30 min north) for budget airlines like Spirit, JetBlue. Hard Rock Stadium is in Miami Gardens, ~16 miles north of downtown Miami. Access via shuttle buses on event days. From airport: Uber $25-35 to Miami Beach, $30-40 to stadium area. Brightline train connects Miami, Fort Lauderdale, West Palm Beach.

- **gettingAround**: Metrorail (1 line, north-south), Metromover (free downtown circulator), Metrobus, Uber/Lyft, trolleys in neighborhoods (free). Miami is car-dependent outside of downtown/Brickell corridor. Renting a car recommended for exploring beyond downtown. For game days: dedicated shuttle buses from downtown hotels and transit hubs to Hard Rock Stadium. Beach parking is expensive ($20-30).

- **neighborhoods**: Little Havana (Cuban heart of Miami, Calle Ocho, must-visit for Latin fans), Brickell (modern, walkable, young professional, bars), Miami Beach/South Beach (iconic, expensive, nightlife, beach), Doral (Venezuelan/Colombian community, "Doralzuela"), Wynwood (arts, murals, trendy). Budget: $60-100 Little Havana/Doral, $100-200 Brickell, $150-400 South Beach.

- **foodAndDrink**: Every Latin American cuisine represented. Cuban: Versailles on Calle Ocho (legendary), La Carreta. Colombian: arepas and bandeja paisa in Doral. Venezuelan: empanadas everywhere. Argentine: parrilladas in Brickell. Peruvian: ceviche in Coral Gables. Haitian: griot in Little Haiti. Cuban coffee (colada/cortadito) is a way of life — $1-2 at ventanitas. Budget: $7-14 casual, $25-60 sit-down in Brickell/Beach.

- **safety**: Tourist areas (South Beach, Brickell, Wynwood, Coconut Grove) are safe. Standard precautions in Overtown, Liberty City late at night. Miami Beach is heavily policed. Watch for petty theft in tourist areas. Emergency: 911. Beach safety: rip currents, hydration.

- **weather**: June-July is summer — hot and humid, 26-33C (79-91F), frequent afternoon thunderstorms (they pass quickly). Hurricane season technically starts June 1 but July storms are rare. Bring sunscreen, light clothes, rain poncho. Mosquitoes are active — bring repellent.

- **culturalContext** (CITY-02): Miami is essentially a Latin American city that happens to be in the US. ~70% Hispanic in Miami-Dade County. Spanish is the dominant language in many areas — you can live in Miami speaking only Spanish. Every Latin American consulate is here. Inter Miami CF (Messi's team) has made the city even more football-obsessed. Calle Ocho in Little Havana is the cultural heartbeat. Latin music (reggaeton, salsa, bachata) everywhere. Tipping: 18-20%. USD is the currency but you'll feel like you're in Latin America.

- **faq**: 5 questions — "Is Miami really that Latin?", "How do I get to Hard Rock Stadium?", "Is South Beach worth the high prices?", "Where should I stay for the best Latin food?", "Is Miami safe for tourists?"

- **sources**: Greater Miami Convention & Visitors Bureau (miamiandbeaches.com), Miami-Dade Transit (miamidade.gov/transit), FIFA.com

**SEATTLE** (~1500 words ES, ~1500 words EN):

- **overview**: Seattle is the largest city in the Pacific Northwest (~4M metro), surrounded by stunning mountains (Mt. Rainier, Cascades, Olympics) and water (Puget Sound). Lumen Field (home of the Seahawks and Sounders FC, 69,000 capacity) will host World Cup matches. The city is famous for coffee culture (Starbucks originated here), tech industry (Amazon, Microsoft), and a passionate soccer culture — the Sounders are one of MLS's most successful clubs with average crowds of 30,000+. Seattle's Hispanic community (~7% city, growing) is smaller than southern US cities but the Sounders fandom is deeply diverse.

- **gettingThere**: Seattle-Tacoma International (SEA) with direct flights from Mexico City and some seasonal Latin American routes. Link Light Rail connects the airport to downtown in 40 minutes ($3.25). Lumen Field is in the SoDo district, south of downtown — walkable from Pioneer Square and International District stations. Amtrak Cascades from Portland (3.5h) and Vancouver BC (4h).

- **gettingAround**: Link Light Rail (expanding, airport to downtown to University), Metro buses, Seattle Streetcar. Downtown and Capitol Hill are walkable. Uber/Lyft. Water taxi to West Seattle. Renting a car is unnecessary for central Seattle. For Lumen Field: walk from downtown (15-20 min) or light rail to Stadium Station. Seattle traffic is notoriously bad — do not drive to the game.

- **neighborhoods**: Capitol Hill (trendy, nightlife, LGBTQ+ friendly, walkable), Pioneer Square (historic, near Lumen Field, budget), Belltown (downtown adjacent, restaurants, bars), International District (Asian food, budget), Fremont/Ballard (quirky, craft breweries). Budget: $60-100 Pioneer Square, $100-180 Capitol Hill/Belltown, $150-300 downtown hotels.

- **foodAndDrink**: Seafood is king — salmon, Dungeness crab, oysters. Pike Place Market is iconic (fish throwing, fresh produce, first Starbucks). Pho and Asian food in the International District. Craft coffee at every corner (not just Starbucks — try Victrola, Elm, Storyville). Craft beer scene is massive. Budget: $10-18 casual, $25-55 sit-down. Pike Place Market for affordable lunch.

- **safety**: Very safe city for tourists. Standard precautions in Pioneer Square and parts of downtown (homelessness is visible but not typically dangerous). Capitol Hill and neighborhoods are very safe. Emergency: 911. Rain-related: sidewalks can be slippery.

- **weather**: June-July is Seattle's BEST season — 15-25C (59-77F), mostly dry and sunny (contrary to the rainy reputation, summers are beautiful). Long daylight hours (15+ hours of light in June). Very comfortable for outdoor activity. Bring layers for cool evenings. Lumen Field is open-air — sunscreen for day games.

- **culturalContext** (CITY-02): Seattle's Latino community is smaller than Texas/California cities but growing. White Center (south Seattle) has a vibrant Mexican/Central American community with taquerias and markets. Burien has a diverse Latino presence. Mexican consulate serves the region. The Sounders FC have an international fanbase and the city embraces soccer culture more than most US cities. Pacific Northwest culture is polite, progressive, and outdoors-oriented. Tipping: 18-20%. People are friendly but reserved (the "Seattle Freeze").

- **faq**: 4 questions — "Does it rain a lot during the World Cup in Seattle?", "How do I get to Lumen Field?", "Is Seattle expensive?", "Where can I find Latin American food?"

- **sources**: Visit Seattle (visitseattle.org), Sound Transit (soundtransit.org), Pike Place Market, FIFA.com

**SAN FRANCISCO BAY AREA** (~1500 words ES, ~1500 words EN):

- **overview**: The San Francisco Bay Area (~7.7M metro) is one of the world's most recognizable regions — the Golden Gate Bridge, Silicon Valley, and incredible cultural diversity. Levi's Stadium in Santa Clara (home of the 49ers, 68,500 capacity) will host World Cup matches. The Bay Area has a large Hispanic community (~24% across the region) with strong Mexican and Central American roots, particularly in San Jose's East Side, the Mission District in SF, and East Oakland. For Latin American fans, the Bay Area offers stunning scenery, world-class food, and a culturally rich experience — though it's one of the most expensive areas in the US.

- **gettingThere**: San Francisco International (SFO) and San Jose International (SJC, closer to Levi's Stadium). SFO has direct flights from Mexico City, Guadalajara, and some South American cities. BART train connects SFO to downtown SF (30 min, $10). Levi's Stadium is in Santa Clara, ~45 miles south of SF. Caltrain + VTA light rail to Great America Station (adjacent to stadium). Or drive on 101/280 (parking available).

- **gettingAround**: BART (Bay Area Rapid Transit, connects SF, Oakland, Berkeley, SFO). Muni buses/streetcars within SF. Caltrain (SF to San Jose/Santa Clara). VTA light rail in South Bay. Uber/Lyft. SF is walkable in many neighborhoods but hilly. For Levi's Stadium: Caltrain + VTA is recommended over driving. Clipper card works across BART, Muni, Caltrain, VTA.

- **neighborhoods**: Mission District SF (Latino cultural heart, murals, taquerias, nightlife), SOMA/Downtown SF (hotels, transit hub), San Jose Downtown (affordable, close to Levi's, tech vibes), Oakland (diverse, food scene, budget), Santa Clara/Sunnyvale (near stadium, suburban). Budget: $60-100 San Jose, $80-150 Oakland, $150-350 SF downtown.

- **foodAndDrink**: Mission District has the best burritos in the world (La Taqueria, El Farolito — the San Francisco Mission burrito is an art form). Dim sum in Chinatown. Seafood at Fisherman's Wharf (touristy but fun). Sourdough bread. Farm-to-table dining in Berkeley. San Jose has excellent Vietnamese and Mexican food. Budget: $10-18 casual, $30-70 sit-down (SF is expensive).

- **safety**: SF: tourist areas (Fisherman's Wharf, Union Square, Mission) are safe. Tenderloin and parts of SOMA have visible homelessness and drug use — avoid walking through at night. Car break-ins are epidemic — NEVER leave ANYTHING visible in a car. San Jose: generally safe. Oakland: varies by neighborhood. Emergency: 911.

- **weather**: June-July in SF is famously foggy and COOL — 13-18C (55-64F). Mark Twain's quote applies. Bring layers and a jacket even in summer. Santa Clara (Levi's Stadium) is significantly warmer — 20-30C (68-86F), sunny. The weather difference between SF and Santa Clara is dramatic. If attending a game at Levi's, dress for warmth.

- **culturalContext** (CITY-02): The Mission District in SF has been the cultural heart of the Bay Area's Latino community for decades. Murals on every wall, Dia de los Muertos celebrations, Mission Street bustling with taquerias and Mexican bakeries. Gentrification has changed the neighborhood but the culture persists. San Jose's East Side has a strong Mexican-American community. Mexican consulate in SF. The San Jose Earthquakes have Latino supporters groups. For Latin American fans, the Mission District will feel like home — and the burritos are genuinely life-changing.

- **faq**: 4 questions — "Is San Francisco really that cold in summer?", "How far is Levi's Stadium from San Francisco?", "Where's the best Mexican food in the Bay Area?", "Is the Bay Area affordable?"

- **sources**: San Francisco Travel (sftravel.com), Visit San Jose (sanjose.org), BART (bart.gov), VTA (vta.org), FIFA.com

**BOSTON** (~1500 words ES, ~1500 words EN):

- **overview**: Boston is a historic New England city (~4.9M metro) known for its colonial history, elite universities (Harvard, MIT), passionate sports culture, and charming architecture. Gillette Stadium in Foxborough (home of the Patriots, 65,000 capacity) is located ~30 miles south of downtown Boston. The city has a growing Latino community (~20% city, primarily Dominican, Puerto Rican, and Central American). Boston is walkable, has excellent transit, and offers a unique American experience blending historical significance with modern energy. For Latin American fans, the city offers cultural richness despite being less Latino-friendly than southern/western US cities.

- **gettingThere**: Boston Logan International (BOS) with direct flights from some Latin American cities and excellent US connections. Silver Line bus connects Logan to downtown (free from airport). Gillette Stadium is in Foxborough — NO direct transit from Boston. Options: drive (45 min), Patriot Place shuttles from select locations, Uber ($60-80). For other cities: Amtrak from NYC (4h), Megabus/FlixBus budget options.

- **gettingAround**: MBTA "The T" subway (4 lines — Red, Green, Orange, Blue), buses, commuter rail. Boston's downtown and Back Bay are very walkable. The city is compact. Uber/Lyft. DO NOT drive in Boston (confusing streets, expensive parking). For Gillette Stadium: the lack of transit is a major issue — plan for driving or organized shuttles. Commuter rail to Foxborough may have special service on game days.

- **neighborhoods**: Back Bay (upscale, Newbury Street, walkable), Beacon Hill (charming, historic), Cambridge (Harvard/MIT, diverse, restaurants), East Boston/Chelsea (Latino neighborhoods, budget, waterfront), South End (trendy, restaurants, diverse). Budget: $50-90 East Boston/Chelsea, $120-250 Back Bay, $100-180 Cambridge.

- **foodAndDrink**: New England clam chowder (the cream-based one, in a bread bowl), lobster rolls, Boston cream pie. East Boston has excellent Latino food — Colombian, Salvadoran, Mexican. North End for Italian. Faneuil Hall Marketplace for variety (touristy). Modern Pastry or Mike's Pastry in North End for cannoli. Budget: $10-16 casual, $25-55 sit-down.

- **safety**: Very safe city for tourists. Standard precautions in parts of Dorchester and Roxbury late at night. The T is safe. Downtown, Back Bay, Cambridge, North End all very safe. Foxborough/Gillette Stadium area is safe but isolated. Emergency: 911.

- **weather**: June-July is Boston's best season — 18-28C (64-82F), generally pleasant with some humidity. Occasional heat waves. Rain possible. Gillette Stadium is open-air. Pack for variable weather — Boston can change quickly.

- **culturalContext** (CITY-02): East Boston (Eastie) has become Boston's Latino hub — Dominican, Colombian, Salvadoran restaurants and businesses line Meridian Street. Chelsea (adjacent, accessible by Silver Line bus) is ~65% Hispanic. Jamaica Plain has a growing diverse community. Mexican and Colombian consulates. New England Revolution (MLS) play at Gillette Stadium. Boston is not as immediately welcoming as southern cities for Spanish speakers — English dominates in most areas outside Eastie/Chelsea. But the communities are there if you know where to look.

- **faq**: 4 questions — "How do I get to Gillette Stadium from downtown Boston?", "Is Boston walkable?", "Where is the Latino community in Boston?", "What should I eat in Boston?"

- **sources**: Visit Boston (bostonusa.com), MBTA (mbta.com), Gillette Stadium (gillettestadium.com), FIFA.com

**KANSAS CITY** (~1500 words ES, ~1500 words EN):

- **overview**: Kansas City straddles the Missouri-Kansas border (~2.2M metro) and is famous for three things: BBQ, jazz, and now soccer. Arrowhead Stadium (home of the Chiefs, 76,000 capacity, one of the loudest stadiums on Earth) will host World Cup matches. KC has emerged as a genuine soccer city — Sporting Kansas City has a passionate fanbase, and the city is building a new CPKC Stadium for the Kansas City Current (women's team). The Hispanic community (~10% metro, primarily Mexican-American) is concentrated in the Kansas City, Kansas (KCK) side, particularly the historic Westside neighborhood. For Latin American fans, KC offers incredible BBQ, genuine midwestern hospitality, and affordable prices.

- **gettingThere**: Kansas City International (MCI) with domestic connections and seasonal Mexico flights. The airport is 20 miles northwest of downtown — Uber $25-35. No direct international flights from Latin America (connect via Dallas, Houston, or Chicago). Amtrak Missouri River Runner from St. Louis (5.5h). Arrowhead Stadium is in the Truman Sports Complex, east of downtown (~15 min drive).

- **gettingAround**: KC Streetcar (free, runs through downtown — expansion to UMKC underway), RideKC buses, Uber/Lyft. Kansas City is car-dependent for most trips. Renting a car is recommended. For Arrowhead Stadium: drive and park ($40-60) or rideshare. No direct transit to the stadium. Downtown is walkable in the core (Power & Light District, River Market, Crossroads).

- **neighborhoods**: Power & Light District (nightlife, entertainment, central), Crossroads Arts District (galleries, restaurants, trendy), Westport (bars, eclectic, young), KCK Westside (Latino neighborhood, authentic Mexican food, murals), River Market (farmers market, restaurants). Budget: $60-100 downtown, $50-80 KCK, $80-150 Crossroads/Westport.

- **foodAndDrink**: BBQ is a way of life — Joe's KC (legendary burnt ends), Q39, Jack Stack, Gates BBQ. KC BBQ uses a thick, sweet tomato-based sauce. KCK Westside has excellent Mexican restaurants — tacos, tamales, menudo at authentic spots. Southwest Boulevard is the Mexican food corridor. Budget: $8-15 casual (BBQ and tacos), $20-40 sit-down.

- **safety**: Very safe city. Downtown and tourist areas are comfortable. KCK Westside: fine during the day, use normal caution at night. Arrowhead Stadium area is safe on event days. Standard midwestern friendliness — people will help you if you look lost. Emergency: 911.

- **weather**: June-July is hot and humid — 25-35C (77-95F) with thunderstorm potential. Arrowhead Stadium is open-air and can be brutally hot in midday sun. Bring sunscreen, hat, water. Tornado season is mostly past by late June, but severe storms are possible.

- **culturalContext** (CITY-02): KCK's Westside neighborhood has been a Mexican-American community since the early 1900s — one of the oldest in the Midwest. The area has murals, quinceañera shops, panaderias, and authentic taquerias. Southwest Boulevard is the commercial corridor. Mexican consulate in KC. Sporting KC has a diverse fanbase with Latino supporters. Midwest hospitality is genuine — Kansas Citians are genuinely friendly and helpful. The city punches above its weight in culture and food for its size.

- **faq**: 4 questions — "How do I get to Arrowhead Stadium?", "What makes KC BBQ special?", "Is Kansas City affordable?", "Where can I find Mexican food in KC?"

- **sources**: Visit KC (visitkc.com), Sporting Kansas City, RideKC (ridekc.org), FIFA.com
