**MONTERREY** (~1500 words ES, ~1500 words EN):

- **overview**: Monterrey is the industrial powerhouse of Mexico, third-largest metro area (~5.3M), nestled dramatically against the Sierra Madre Oriental. Known for its entrepreneurial spirit, excellent carne asada culture, and the iconic Cerro de la Silla silhouette. The Estadio BBVA (home of Rayados) is one of the most modern stadiums in the Americas. The city has a distinct northern Mexican identity — more Tex-Mex influenced, business-oriented, with hot summers. For Latin American fans, Monterrey is one of the easiest cities to reach from Mexico City (~1.5h flight) and has a proud, passionate futbol culture.

- **gettingThere**: Aeropuerto Internacional de Monterrey (MTY), also known as Mariano Escobedo. Direct flights from Mexico City (1.5h), Guadalajara (1.5h), Houston (1.5h), Dallas (2h). First-class bus options: ETN, Primera Plus from CDMX (~9 hours). The airport is 24km from downtown — Uber/taxi to Centro ~$15 USD.

- **gettingAround**: Metrorrey (2 lines, basic but functional), Ecovia BRT, Uber/DiDi very popular, driving culture is strong (city is spread out). Ruta buses for budget travel. The city is NOT walkable in most areas — you need transport between zones. Estadio BBVA is in Guadalupe, east of downtown (~20 min by car).

- **neighborhoods**: San Pedro Garza Garcia (upscale, best restaurants, safe), Barrio Antiguo (Centro, nightlife, bohemian), Fundidora Park area (modern, cultural), Cumbres/Valle (suburban, residential, malls). Budget: $30-60/night Centro, $80-200 San Pedro.

- **foodAndDrink**: Carne asada regia is legendary — cabrito (baby goat), machitos, frijoles charros. Try at La Nacional, El Gran Pastor. Craft beer scene is excellent (Cerveceria Allende, Fauna). Budget street food: $3-7 USD/meal. Sit-down: $12-25 USD. Must-try: cabrito al pastor, discada.

- **safety**: Generally safe in tourist/commercial areas. Avoid isolated colonias at night. Use Uber over street taxis. San Pedro is one of the safest municipalities in Mexico. Emergency: 911. Tourist areas in Barrio Antiguo have police presence.

- **weather**: June-July is HOT — 32-40C (90-104F). Occasional thunderstorms. Bring sunscreen, hat, light clothing. Stay hydrated. Indoor attractions for midday heat.

- **culturalContext** (CITY-02): Northern Mexican culture is distinct — more reserved than central Mexico, strong work ethic, proud regional identity. Spanish is universal. The city has fans of both Rayados (Monterrey) and Tigres UNAM — intense local rivalry. Mexican consulates for other Latin American nationals. Tipping: 10-15%. Peso accepted everywhere.

- **faq**: 4 questions — "Is Monterrey safe for tourists?", "How hot does it get in June?", "How do I get to Estadio BBVA from downtown?", "Is there a Latino fan community in Monterrey?"

- **sources**: Visit Monterrey (turismo.monterrey.gob.mx), FIFA.com

**GUADALAJARA** (~1500 words ES, ~1500 words EN):

- **overview**: Guadalajara is Mexico's second-largest city (~5.2M metro), the cultural capital of western Mexico, and birthplace of mariachi, tequila, and the charro tradition. Estadio Akron (home of Chivas) will host World Cup matches. The city blends colonial architecture with modern innovation (it's Mexico's tech hub). Nearby Tequila and Lake Chapala are easy day trips. The climate is milder than Monterrey. For Latin American fans, Guadalajara represents the soul of Mexican culture.

- **gettingThere**: Aeropuerto Internacional de Guadalajara Miguel Hidalgo (GDL). Direct flights from CDMX (1h), Monterrey (1.5h), Houston (2.5h), LA (3h), Dallas (2.5h). ETN/Primera Plus buses from CDMX (~6h). Airport is 17km south — Uber to Centro ~$10 USD.

- **gettingAround**: Mi Macro light rail (2 lines), Mi Macro Periferico BRT, Uber/DiDi, taxis de sitio. The Centro Historico is walkable. Tlaquepaque and Zapopan require transport. Estadio Akron is in Zapopan, west of city (~30 min from Centro).

- **neighborhoods**: Centro Historico (colonial, budget, walkable), Chapultepec/Americana (trendy, cafes, nightlife), Zapopan Centro (cultural, quieter), Providencia (upscale, dining). Budget: $25-50/night Centro, $50-120 Chapultepec, $100-200 Providencia.

- **foodAndDrink**: Birria (signature dish), tortas ahogadas, jericalla (dessert), tejuino (corn drink). Mercado San Juan de Dios (largest covered market in Latin America). Tequila tastings everywhere. Budget: $4-8 USD street food, $12-25 sit-down. Must-try: birria at Birrieria las 9 Esquinas.

- **safety**: Safe in tourist areas. Standard precautions in Centro at night. Chapultepec/Americana very safe. Use Uber. Emergency: 911.

- **weather**: June-July is rainy season but milder than CDMX — 18-30C (64-86F). Afternoon showers. Pleasant mornings. Pack layers and rain jacket.

- **culturalContext** (CITY-02): Guadalajara is deeply traditional Mexican culture — mariachi originated here, Chivas de Guadalajara is Mexico's most popular team (only Mexican players allowed). Strong Catholic traditions, vibrant arts scene. Jalisco is welcoming to visitors. The city has a growing international community. Day trip to Tequila (1h) is unmissable.

- **faq**: 4 questions — "Should I visit the town of Tequila?", "How do I get to Estadio Akron?", "What is birria?", "Is Guadalajara walkable?"

- **sources**: Visit Guadalajara (visitguadalajara.com), Jalisco Tourism, FIFA.com

**NEW YORK / NEW JERSEY** (~1500 words ES, ~1500 words EN):

- **overview**: The NY/NJ metropolitan area is the largest in the US (~20M). MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, NJ will host World Cup matches including potentially the final or semifinal. The region is a global melting pot with enormous Latin American communities — Jackson Heights (Queens) is one of the most diverse neighborhoods on Earth. For Spanish-speaking fans, this region feels surprisingly welcoming with Spanish spoken widely in Queens, the Bronx, and northern NJ cities like Union City ("Havana on the Hudson").

- **gettingThere**: Three major airports — JFK (main international), Newark EWR (closest to MetLife Stadium, 15 min), LaGuardia LGA (domestic). Direct flights from every Latin American capital. MetLife Stadium is in East Rutherford, NJ — reachable via NJ Transit from Penn Station or dedicated shuttle buses during events. Amtrak from Philadelphia (1.5h), Boston (3.5h), DC (3h).

- **gettingAround**: NYC subway (24/7, $2.90/ride, OMNY contactless). NJ Transit trains/buses for MetLife Stadium access. Uber/Lyft. Walking is the best way to explore Manhattan. DO NOT rent a car in Manhattan (parking is $40-80/day). MetLife Stadium parking is massive but traffic is brutal — use transit.

- **neighborhoods**: Jackson Heights, Queens (budget, incredible Latin food, feels like home for Latin Americans), Midtown Manhattan (convenient, expensive, Times Square), Brooklyn (trendy, diverse, Williamsburg/DUMBO), Jersey City/Union City NJ (budget, close to MetLife, Spanish-speaking communities). Budget: $50-100/night Queens, $150-400 Manhattan, $80-150 Jersey City.

- **foodAndDrink**: Every cuisine on Earth available. For Latin American fans: Jackson Heights has Colombian, Mexican, Ecuadorian, Peruvian restaurants. Elmhurst for tacos. Halal carts for $5 meals. Pizza by the slice ($3-5). Arepas at Arepa Lady. Budget: $8-15/meal street/casual, $25-60 sit-down. NYC is expensive — eat where locals eat, not tourist traps.

- **safety**: NYC is statistically very safe for tourists (crime at historic lows). Standard precautions: avoid empty subway cars late at night, keep valuables close, be aware in crowded tourist areas (pickpockets). Times Square is safe but full of scams. Emergency: 911. NYPD has tourist units.

- **weather**: June-July is hot and humid — 25-32C (77-90F). Occasional heat waves to 38C. Afternoon thunderstorms possible. Bring light, breathable clothing. AC is everywhere indoors. Hydrate constantly.

Jackson Heights Queens has Colombian, Mexican, Ecuadorian communities with fully Spanish-speaking businesses. Union City NJ is ~65% Hispanic. For Latin American fans, this city does NOT feel foreign — your language and culture are everywhere in Queens.

- **overview**: Los Angeles is the World Cup 2026 showpiece city — SoFi Stadium in Inglewood will host the final. LA is America's second-largest city (~4M city, 13M metro) and arguably the most Latino major city in the US (~48% Hispanic). Spanish is spoken everywhere — from East LA to the Westside. The city offers beaches, Hollywood, incredible Mexican food, and year-round sunshine. For Latin American fans, LA feels like a second home.

- **gettingThere**: LAX is the main airport (international). Direct flights from every major Latin American city. Volaris and VivaAerobus offer budget flights from Mexico. SoFi Stadium is in Inglewood, 5 miles from LAX (~15 min). Other airports: Long Beach (LGB), Burbank (BUR), Ontario (ONT). Amtrak Pacific Surfliner from San Diego (2.5h). Greyhound/FlixBus from other CA cities.

- **gettingAround**: LA is a CAR CITY — traffic is legendary. Metro rail (7 lines, expanding rapidly), Metro bus, Uber/Lyft. New Metro K Line connects LAX area to SoFi Stadium vicinity. Renting a car is practical for exploring beyond downtown but parking at SoFi is limited — use transit or rideshare for game days. Walking is viable in Santa Monica, DTLA, Hollywood only.

- **neighborhoods**: East LA/Boyle Heights (Latino heart of LA, authentic Mexican food, budget), DTLA Downtown (hotels, nightlife, arts), Santa Monica/Venice (beach, expensive, tourist-friendly), Koreatown (diverse, central, good transit, budget), Inglewood (near SoFi, rapidly developing, affordable). Budget: $60-100 East LA/Inglewood, $100-200 DTLA, $200-400 Santa Monica.

- **foodAndDrink**: LA has the best Mexican food outside of Mexico. Tacos: Leo's Tacos (al pastor), Guerrilla Tacos, Mariscos Jalisco (shrimp tacos). Oaxacan food in Koreatown. Grand Central Market for variety. Jonathan Gold's legacy: explore every neighborhood. Budget: $5-12 street/casual, $20-50 sit-down. Taco trucks are everywhere and outstanding.

- **safety**: Safe in tourist areas. Exercise caution in Skid Row (DTLA), parts of South LA, late night in Hollywood. Uber over walking long distances at night. Car break-ins are common — never leave valuables visible. Beach areas very safe. Emergency: 911.

- **weather**: June-July is perfect LA weather — 20-28C (68-82F), almost no rain, sunny. Coastal areas cooler (marine layer morning fog). Inland valleys hotter. Sunscreen essential. Bring layers for evening ocean breeze.

Olvera Street is the historic Mexican marketplace. Spanish-language media, businesses, and services are ubiquitous. Mexican consulate (largest in the world) in DTLA. LAFC and LA Galaxy have passionate Latino fanbases. Mariachi Plaza in Boyle Heights for live music. For Mexican and Central American fans, LA will feel like home.
