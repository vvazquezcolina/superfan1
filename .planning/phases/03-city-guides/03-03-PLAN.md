**DALLAS** (~1500 words ES, ~1500 words EN):

- **overview**: Dallas-Fort Worth is the fourth-largest metro in the US (~7.6M). AT&T Stadium in Arlington (between Dallas and Fort Worth) is a colossal 80,000-seat venue with a retractable roof and the iconic massive video screen. The DFW region has one of the fastest-growing Hispanic populations in the US (~40% of the metro is Hispanic). Texas culture meets Mexico here — Tex-Mex food is a religion. For Latin American fans, the DFW area is accessible (major international hub airport), affordable compared to coastal cities, and deeply connected to Mexican culture.

- **gettingThere**: DFW International Airport (DFW) is one of the busiest in the world with direct flights from Mexico City, Monterrey, Guadalajara, Cancun, Bogota, Lima, and most US cities. Dallas Love Field (DAL) for Southwest Airlines. AT&T Stadium is in Arlington — 20 miles from both DFW airport and downtown Dallas. No direct transit to AT&T Stadium — Uber/rideshare ($25-35 from downtown) or dedicated event shuttle buses. Amtrak Texas Eagle from Chicago, San Antonio.

- **gettingAround**: DART light rail (4 lines, connects Dallas to many suburbs but NOT Arlington). TRE commuter rail to Fort Worth. Uber/Lyft essential for AT&T Stadium access. Renting a car is practical — Texas is built for driving. Toll roads are common (TollTag or pay-by-mail). Downtown Dallas is walkable in the core. Arlington has NO public transit.

- **neighborhoods**: Uptown Dallas (trendy, walkable, nightlife), Deep Ellum (arts, music, bars), Oak Cliff/Bishop Arts (Latino neighborhood, great Mexican food, murals), Downtown Fort Worth (Stockyards, western culture), Arlington (near stadium, suburban, hotels). Budget: $50-90/night Arlington, $80-150 Uptown, $60-100 Fort Worth.

- **foodAndDrink**: Tex-Mex is king — breakfast tacos, queso, enchiladas, fajitas. BBQ is the other religion (brisket at Pecan Lodge, Terry Black's). Oak Cliff has authentic Mexican food. Pepe's & Mito's for Tex-Mex institution. Budget: $8-15 casual, $20-45 sit-down. Street tacos at gas stations are surprisingly great in Texas.

- **safety**: Generally safe in tourist/commercial areas. Standard urban precautions in Deep Ellum late at night. Arlington stadium area is very safe on event days with heavy police presence. Texas heat is the main health risk — stay hydrated.

- **weather**: June-July is HOT — 32-40C (90-104F). Humid. Occasional severe thunderstorms. AT&T Stadium has a roof (air-conditioned). Bring sunscreen, hat, light clothing. Drink water constantly. Heat stroke is a real risk outdoors.

- **culturalContext** (CITY-02): The DFW metro has ~2M Hispanic residents. Oak Cliff is a vibrant Mexican-American neighborhood. Spanish is widely spoken in many areas. Mexican consulate in Dallas. The area has a massive FC Dallas fanbase with Latino supporters groups. Rodeo culture meets futbol culture in Texas. Tipping: 18-20% expected in restaurants. Cowboys and futbol fans coexist peacefully.

- **faq**: 4 questions — "How do I get to AT&T Stadium without a car?", "Is Dallas or Fort Worth better for hotels?", "How hot is Dallas in summer?", "Where do Latino fans gather in DFW?"

- **sources**: Visit Dallas (visitdallas.com), Visit Fort Worth (fortworth.com), DART (dart.org), FIFA.com

**HOUSTON** (~1500 words ES, ~1500 words EN):

- **overview**: Houston is the fourth-largest US city (~2.3M city, 7M+ metro) and has the most diverse population of any major US city. NRG Stadium (home of the Texans, 72,000 capacity) will host World Cup matches. Houston has a MASSIVE Hispanic community (~45% of the city), making it one of the most Spanish-friendly US cities. The city is a global energy capital, home to NASA's Johnson Space Center, and has an incredible food scene that rivals NYC. For Latin American fans, Houston is a gateway city with direct flights from all over Latin America and a culture that already feels like home.

- **gettingThere**: George Bush Intercontinental (IAH) is United Airlines' hub with direct flights from Mexico City, Monterrey, Guadalajara, Bogota, Lima, Sao Paulo, Buenos Aires, and most Latin American capitals. William P. Hobby (HOU) for Southwest/budget airlines. NRG Stadium is in the NRG Park complex, 10 miles south of downtown. Megabus/Greyhound from Dallas (4h), San Antonio (3h), Austin (2.5h).

- **gettingAround**: METRORail light rail (3 lines, connects downtown to NRG Park — this is KEY for game day access). Metro bus network. Uber/Lyft. Houston is huge and car-dependent for most trips. Renting a car is practical but parking at NRG is expensive ($40+). For game days: METRORail Red Line goes directly to NRG Park. Downtown is partially walkable.

- **neighborhoods**: Midtown (central, walkable, diverse nightlife), East Downtown/EaDo (trendy, near METRORail, good hotels), Westchase/Hillcroft (Little India meets Latin America, budget), Bellaire Chinatown/Gulfton (incredibly diverse, super affordable, amazing ethnic food), The Heights (trendy, bars, restaurants). Budget: $50-80 Gulfton/Westchase, $80-150 Midtown/EaDo, $100-200 downtown hotels.

- **foodAndDrink**: Houston's food scene is elite and diverse. Tex-Mex: El Tiempo, The Original Ninfa's (invented fajitas). Vietnamese: Pho Binh on Bellaire. BBQ: Truth BBQ, Killen's. Kolaches (Czech-Texan pastry). Budget: $8-15 casual, $20-50 sit-down. Taco trucks on every corner. Montrose area for trendy dining.

- **safety**: Safe in tourist areas. Standard precautions in Third Ward, parts of south Houston late at night. NRG Park area is safe on event days. Houston has its own police horse patrol in tourist zones. Main risk: heat and flooding (Houston floods easily in heavy rain).

- **weather**: June-July is hot AND humid — 27-35C (80-95F) with 80%+ humidity. Feels like 40C+ (104F+). Afternoon thunderstorms common (Houston floods — carry an umbrella). NRG Stadium is domed with AC. Bring the lightest clothing you own. Hydrate obsessively.

- **culturalContext** (CITY-02): Houston is arguably the most Latin American-friendly city in the US after Miami. ~45% Hispanic, predominantly Mexican-American but with large Salvadoran, Honduran, Colombian, and Venezuelan communities. Spanish is spoken in vast areas of the city — entire neighborhoods operate in Spanish. Mexican consulate plus consulates for most Latin American nations. Houston Dynamo has passionate Latino supporters. The Gulfton area is sometimes called "Little Central America." For fans from Mexico or Central America, Houston will feel completely natural.

- **faq**: 5 questions — "How do I get to NRG Stadium?", "Is Houston walkable?", "How do I deal with the heat and humidity?", "Where are the best Latino neighborhoods?", "What's the food scene like?"

- **sources**: Visit Houston (visithoustontexas.com), Houston Metro (ridemetro.org), NASA Johnson Space Center, FIFA.com

**ATLANTA** (~1500 words ES, ~1500 words EN):

- **overview**: Atlanta is the capital of Georgia and the cultural hub of the southeastern US (~6M metro). Mercedes-Benz Stadium (home of Atlanta United FC, 71,000 capacity) is renowned for its unique 8-petal retractable roof and is one of the most technologically advanced stadiums in the world. Atlanta's Hispanic community is growing rapidly (~12% metro) with significant Mexican, Guatemalan, and Colombian populations concentrated in Buford Highway corridor. The city is also a major hub for Black culture, music (hip-hop capital), and civil rights history. For Latin American fans, Atlanta is affordable, has direct international flights, and offers a unique Southern US experience.

- **gettingThere**: Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International (ATL) is the busiest airport in the WORLD with direct flights from Mexico City, Bogota, Lima, and many US cities. MARTA rail connects the airport directly to downtown in 20 minutes ($2.50). Mercedes-Benz Stadium is in downtown Atlanta, walkable from several MARTA stations (Vine City or GWCC/CNN Center). Amtrak Crescent from DC (9h) and New Orleans (11h).

- **gettingAround**: MARTA rail (4 lines, covers downtown/airport/Buckhead). MARTA bus. Uber/Lyft. Atlanta Streetcar in downtown. The city is car-dependent for areas outside the MARTA corridor. Renting a car useful for Buford Highway food corridor. Mercedes-Benz Stadium is one of the most transit-accessible NFL/MLS venues — MARTA is the smart choice on game day.

- **neighborhoods**: Downtown (walkable to stadium, hotels, Centennial Olympic Park), Midtown (arts, parks, Piedmont Park), Buckhead (upscale, malls, nightlife), Buford Highway corridor (international food mecca, diverse, budget), Decatur (charming, restaurants). Budget: $60-100 downtown, $80-150 Midtown, $50-80 Buford Highway area.

- **foodAndDrink**: Southern cuisine: fried chicken, biscuits, peach cobbler, grits. Buford Highway is Atlanta's crown jewel — 10+ miles of every Asian and Latin American cuisine. Plaza Fiesta mall has Mexican food vendors. Ponce City Market for trendy eats. Waffle House is a Georgia institution (24/7, cheap). Budget: $7-12 casual, $18-40 sit-down.

- **safety**: Downtown and Midtown are safe for tourists. Standard precautions at night in areas west of downtown. MARTA is safe but be aware on late-night trains. Mercedes-Benz Stadium area has heavy security on event days. Emergency: 911.

- **weather**: June-July is hot and humid — 25-35C (77-95F) with high humidity. Afternoon thunderstorms common. Mercedes-Benz Stadium has a retractable roof with AC. Bring light clothes, sunscreen. The humidity can be oppressive for those from dry/cool climates.

- **culturalContext** (CITY-02): Atlanta's Latino community is concentrated along the Buford Highway corridor (Chamblee, Doraville). Plaza Fiesta is a Mexican-style shopping mall. Spanish-language churches, radio stations, and businesses serve the community. Mexican and Guatemalan consulates in Atlanta. Atlanta United FC has one of the most passionate fanbases in MLS — the Resurgence and Terminus Legion supporters groups include many Latino members. Southern hospitality is real — Atlantans are generally friendly to visitors.

- **faq**: 4 questions — "How do I get to Mercedes-Benz Stadium?", "Is MARTA safe and convenient?", "Where can I find Latin American food?", "What's the weather like in summer?"

- **sources**: Discover Atlanta (discoveratlanta.com), MARTA (itsmarta.com), Mercedes-Benz Stadium, FIFA.com

**PHILADELPHIA** (~1500 words ES, ~1500 words EN):

- **overview**: Philadelphia is America's sixth-largest city (~1.6M, 6.2M metro), the birthplace of the nation (Declaration of Independence signed here), and a city with incredible history, food, and sports culture. Lincoln Financial Field (home of the Eagles, 69,000 capacity) will host World Cup matches. Philadelphia has a significant and growing Latino community (~15% city, primarily Puerto Rican and Mexican) concentrated in North Philadelphia. The city is affordable for the Northeast US, walkable, and located between NYC and DC. For Latin American fans, Philly offers a genuine, unpretentious American experience with real neighborhoods and passionate sports fans.

- **gettingThere**: Philadelphia International (PHL) has direct flights from some Latin American cities (Cancun, some seasonal routes) and excellent domestic connections. Newark (EWR, 1.5h drive) has more Latin American routes. Amtrak Northeast Regional from NYC (1.5h, ~$30-60), DC (2h, ~$40-70). Megabus/FlixBus options. Lincoln Financial Field is in South Philadelphia Sports Complex — reachable via Broad Street Line subway from Center City (15 min, $2.50).

- **gettingAround**: SEPTA subway (Broad Street Line, Market-Frankford Line), buses, trolleys. The Broad Street Line goes directly to the Sports Complex (NRG/AT&T Station). Center City is very walkable. Uber/Lyft. Renting a car is unnecessary in Center City. Indego bike share available.

- **neighborhoods**: Center City (walkable, hotels, attractions, Rittenhouse Square), South Philadelphia (Italian Market, cheesesteaks, near stadium), North Philadelphia/Kensington (Latino neighborhoods, authentic food, budget — some areas need caution), Old City (historic, Independence Hall, charming), University City (budget, near UPenn/Drexel). Budget: $50-90 South Philly, $100-200 Center City, $40-70 University City.

- **foodAndDrink**: Cheesesteaks (Pat's vs Geno's debate — locals prefer Jim's or John's Roast Pork). Reading Terminal Market is a must (best food hall in America). Italian Market on 9th Street. Soft pretzels (Philly staple). North Philly has excellent Mexican taquerias and Puerto Rican food. Water ice (Italian ice) everywhere in summer. Budget: $6-12 casual, $18-40 sit-down.

- **safety**: Center City and tourist areas are safe. Caution in parts of North Philadelphia and Kensington (drug-affected areas). South Philly near the stadium is fine. SEPTA is generally safe. Emergency: 911. Philly has a rougher reputation than it deserves — tourist areas are very safe.

- **weather**: June-July is warm and humid — 23-32C (73-90F). Occasional heat waves. Rain possible. Lincoln Financial Field is open-air — bring sunscreen, hat, rain poncho just in case. Evenings can be pleasant.

- **culturalContext** (CITY-02): Philadelphia has one of the largest Puerto Rican communities in the US, concentrated in North Philadelphia (El Bloque de Oro on North 5th Street). Growing Mexican community in South Philly. Mexican consulate in Center City. The Feria del Barrio is an annual Latino cultural festival. Spanish-language services available in hospitals and government offices. Philadelphia Union (MLS) plays in Chester but the city's sports fans are legendarily passionate.

- **faq**: 4 questions — "How do I get to Lincoln Financial Field?", "What's the difference between Pat's and Geno's cheesesteaks?", "Is Philadelphia safe?", "How far is Philly from NYC?"

- **sources**: Visit Philadelphia (visitphilly.com), SEPTA (septa.org), Independence Visitor Center, FIFA.com
